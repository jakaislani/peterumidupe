Maya Jama biography

Jama’s father spent the majority of her childhood in prison for violent crimes such as pub brawls and fights. She decided to stop visiting her father in prison when she was 12 years old, and she did so for a decade.

In a 2017 interview with The Guardian, she stated that she had not noticed a problem. In 2017, while working on the documentary When Dads Kill, which dealt with young people’s experiences similar to her own, she relented and examined her relationship with her own father, who had been released from prison at the time, meeting with him in an attempt to elicit answers directly from him as to why he had ended up in prison.

In 2018, a tweet from Jama when she was 17 surfaced, detailing her laughter at a tweet mocking dark-skinned black women. She apologized after being accused of colorism, saying she was “an ignorant, stupid child” when she made the social media post in 2012.

Maya Jama career

Maya Jama relocated to London in order to pursue a career in the media as an actress, TV presenter, and fashion model. Jama had originally wanted to be an actress, but she later realized that she might be more comfortable as a presenter. Early role models for her included June Sarpong and Davina McCall.

When she was a teenager, she made her presenting debut by hosting the weekly music video countdown on JumpOff.TV. She later worked for Sky UK on TRACE Sports.

She served as the host of Maya’s FIFA World Cup Cities for Copa90 in 2014, a travelogue focusing on the Brazil 2014 FIFA World Cup. She also shared hosting duties for the nine-part World Cup Taxi series on Copa90. Jama began working for MTV as a presenter for The Wrap Up in August 2014.

When Life Gives You Melons was a podcast hosted by Jama in 2018 that was made available on streaming services in collaboration with Freya Lingerie. In the podcast, Jama conducted all-female interviews with guests to discuss relationships, work, and life. Vick Hope, Cherry Healey, Anna Richardson, Vanessa White, Katie Piper, Gemma Cairney, and Laura Whitmore were among the visitors.

The same year, Jama accepted a position as an ambassador for Savera UK, a British organization that offers assistance to members of black ethnic minority communities who are at risk of domestic abuse. She gave blood for an NHS campaign in 2019, and it was discovered that she had the uncommon and sought-after Ro blood subtype.

With PrettyLittleThing, she also introduced a successful clothing line in June 2018 that was followed by a second line in February 2019.

She represents the Australian hair care company Aussie and is an ambassador for Adidas. She also participated in the European JD Sports Christmas campaign. She additionally participated in a global GAP campaign and a Kurt Geiger campaign for the spring/summer of 2019.

The first release of Jama’s face and eye mask line, MIJ Masks, sold out within 24 hours of its launch in December 2020.
